Thanks for your effort in writing this book. I’ve lived my whole life thinking writing was a solo pursuit that was supposed to be painful. So, I believed, why would anyone choose an occupation that (previously) promised just more of that pain? This book flips the script. The proverbial Book is supposed to be about reverie and joy, or at least we have the choice to make it so. But so many books have been so harsh on us as readers (and therefore writers), and given us all sorts of wrong impressions. I work in a rigorous scientific profession (and, by coincidence, actually work closely with deers!) and this instructional book is giving me hope that, after I write my first novel (following the terroir project writing challenge), even if I’m not published, I will be a better worker in my chosen field. Because through the process of writing I’ll know so much more about the terroir that makes all of the beautiful animals that I work with so sick (I work with very sick deer, actually). The best advice I have gleaned from Wonderbook and elsewhere is to make sure you keep balance (in your life AND your story – with generosity and collaboration as your guiding stars and counterbalances). And also, to remember the reason you/we/I started out (or, as in my case, to rethink your prior reasoning and how it relates to your life/story after reading Wonderbook).

Wonderment can be an intense experience, but Jeff and Ann and the whole gang have made it colourful, gentle and fun. So, thank you again guys.

Now to find some curious little marmots to help eachother write stories and make sure eachother is on the right Path.

The Crimes of Wade Steven Wilson

Wade Steven Wilson is a name associated with a violent series of events in Florida. In 2019, Wilson was arrested and charged with the brutal murders of two women, which shocked and disturbed the local community and drew significant media attention. Both murders were highly publicized and cast a spotlight on Wilson’s background, as well as on systemic concerns surrounding crime and recidivism.

Connection to Jesse Tehonica and the Fundraising Campaign

In the wake of Wilson’s crimes, a woman named Jesse Tehonica appeared to have developed a personal connection with him. Tehonica set up a Fundly account, aiming to raise money to support his legal defense or related expenses. Unexpectedly, the campaign gained traction, attracting substantial donations that eventually reached close to $100,000. The reasons behind this public interest and fundraising success remain unclear but likely involve a combination of shock, fascination, and the public’s ongoing complex reactions to crime and punishment.

Monica Morrow and the Alleged Impersonation Scheme

Following the success of the fundraising campaign, reports indicate that Tehonica and Wilson became entangled in a scam allegedly orchestrated by Monica Morrow, a woman from North Carolina. Morrow reportedly approached Tehonica while assuming a false identity—a scheme she had reportedly been carrying out for years. For this particular scam, Morrow impersonated Arielle Reitsma, a known model and actress.

Morrow’s impersonation of Reitsma is said to have spanned years, during which time she allegedly fabricated an entire persona as “Amanda Roadmen.” This false identity enabled Morrow to weave herself into others’ lives, cultivating trust through her deceptive identity.

Unraveling the Scam and the Aftermath

Once it became evident that Morrow was engaged in impersonation and fraudulent activity, many individuals associated with the case were left feeling betrayed and manipulated. The implications of this situation shed light on the prevalence and severity of online scams, particularly those involving impersonation, which can exploit vulnerable individuals and affect those connected to high-profile criminal cases.

The public outrage stemming from this saga emphasizes the importance of due diligence in online fundraising efforts, as well as the dangers of impersonation and fraud in digital spaces. The financial and emotional toll on those involved in the case has only underscored how scammers can prey on people’s trust and emotions, especially during moments of vulnerability.

The Broader Impact and Key Takeaways

This case involving Wilson, Tehonica, and Morrow highlights several key issues:

Online Security: Platforms such as Fundly must implement strict safeguards to prevent fraudulent campaigns or impersonation scams, offering donors and beneficiaries more security.
Victim Vulnerability: Victims of fraud, particularly those linked to high-profile cases, are often left without recourse when scammers target them in moments of emotional or financial need.
Psychological Impact of Crime: Cases like Wilson’s and the surrounding publicity can attract individuals seeking connection or involvement, sometimes drawing people who have ulterior motives or aim to exploit these situations.

As this case continues to unfold, it serves as a reminder of the complexity and risk surrounding online interactions and the importance of vigilance when donating or engaging in digital spaces. It also calls attention to the ongoing need for enhanced cybersecurity and anti-fraud measures to protect against similar situations in the future.
